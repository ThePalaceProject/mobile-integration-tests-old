Entrepreneurship is an exercise in disciplined risk-taking. Starting and running your own business requires you to put your own time and money at stake, which can result in failure and loss—or in great success and reward. The difference between those two outcomes comes not from knowing how to avoid risk, but from learning what type of risk to take on and how to leverage it to your greatest benefit.

Risk comes in two forms. Negative risks are those that have the potential to harm your company. For example, lax cybersecurity could result in the high costs of recovering from a cyberattack. Positive risks, on the other hand, have the potential to benefit your company. Whenever you invest time or money in your company, you are taking a positive risk. Naturally, the two types of risk require contrasting approaches.

Negative risks

The instinctive response to negative risks is to avoid them at all costs. That response, however, can be costlier than necessary and can even exacerbate the threat. Instead, evaluate the risk and decide on the best strategy to use against it. Here are four strategies to deal with threats and risks that may have a negative impact on your business:

  • Avoid – Eliminate the threat or protect the organization. Design the risk out of your processes.
  • Transfer – Shift the impact of the threat onto a third party. Contract, outsource, or purchase insurance.
  • Mitigate – Accept the risk, but reduce its potential impact or the probability of its occurrence.
  • Accept – Acknowledge the risk, but do not take any action unless it materializes. This is useful where it is not cost-effective to address specific risks in any other way.

Choose a strategy that matches the risk’s probability and potential impact on your organization. Avoidance and mitigation strategies are usually for critical risks with high impact, while transference and acceptance may be viable strategies for threats with less harmful consequences and that would have a low overall impact on your business.

Positive risks

As an entrepreneur, you likely see opportunity in positive risk all around you. Here are four strategies for dealing with risks that may have a positive impact on your organization:

  • Exploit – Actively pursue risk on your own in order to achieve a return on your capital.
  • Enhance – Work to increase the probability of a risk opportunity presenting itself.
  • Share – Allocate partial ownership of a risk opportunity to a third party who is best able to leverage the opportunity for the benefit of your business.
  • Accept – Take advantage of risk opportunities as they arise, but do not actively seek them out.

The instinct to avoid risk altogether is an innate human trait. Being a successful entrepreneur, however, requires much more than just eliminating risk. It requires the astute ability to identify a risk as either a threat or an opportunity, and then to either minimize its impact or to exploit it for all t is worth
